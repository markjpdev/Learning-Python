## My Take

Coming back to coding after 15 years is a trip. I used to work in C, C++, VB 6.0, and VB.NET where you needed imports, declarations, and proper structure before writing a single line of logic. Python threw all of that out.

One line. No setup. It just works.

That simplicity was actually confusing at first. I kept waiting for the boilerplate that never came. No intellisense warnings, no type declarations, no semicolons. Just write and run. It took a few exercises before it stopped feeling wrong and started feeling right.
